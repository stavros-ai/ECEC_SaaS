SelfAssessPro is a web platform for Australian childcare centres that helps educators turn their everyday practices into government-required documentation. It has three main tools: an NQF Mapping Studio that links what you do to the official quality checklist, a Key Practice Writing Studio that writes polished paragraphs about your practice, and a Quality Improvement Planning Assistant that helps you set goals and action plans. Everything saves into a private library per centre.

SelfAssessPro

A web platform that helps childcare centres turn what they actually do every day into the documentation the government requires — quickly, correctly, and without losing their minds.

1 The Problem

Every childcare centre in Australia has to prove they're meeting quality standards. They do this through Self-Assessment documents and a Quality Improvement Plan. Writing these is hard, slow, and frustrating.

Time-poor

Educators are busy looking after children. Documentation gets pushed to nights, weekends, or just doesn't get done properly.

Knowledge gap

Most educators can talk about what they do, but struggle to connect their practice to the specific elements of the official quality checklist (NQF).

No tools exist

There is no software built for this. Centres use Word docs, spreadsheets, or expensive consultants. None of it scales.

Documents gather dust

Self-assessments are supposed to be living documents, updated regularly. In practice, they're written once and forgotten until the next assessment visit.

You provide

A description of something your centre does. For example: "We have a bush tucker garden that children help maintain, and we use it to teach about Indigenous food systems."

AI returns
  • Best-fit Quality Area(s) and Element(s)
  • A short rationale explaining why
  • A suggested Key Practice idea you can use straight away

You can then save this mapping to your library or jump straight into the Key Practice Writer with the info pre-filled.

You provide
  • The NQF Element (or let AI suggest one)
  • What your centre actually does (the embedded practice)
  • Optionally: how it was informed by reflection, families, or community
AI returns
  • One polished paragraph, max 480 characters
  • Written in first-person plural ("we")
  • Strictly based on what you provided — no fabrication
  • You can regenerate, edit, and refine before saving

After saving, you can view "Assessment Readiness Prompts" — what an assessor might ask, want to see, or observe.

You provide
  • The issue or improvement area you've identified
  • Optionally: the relevant Quality Area, family/community input
AI returns
  • A clear goal statement
  • 5–10 suggested next steps
  • A success measure, priority level, and timeframe
  • Progress notes field you can update over time

4 Who Uses It

Centre / Service Account

Multiple educators contribute under one service. Educators write Key Practices that sit in "pending" until the Director reviews and approves. Shared library, admin dashboard, unlimited users per subscription.

Individual Account

A single educator or leader with their own private account. All data stays private. Monthly or annual billing. Good for consultants or individual professionals.
